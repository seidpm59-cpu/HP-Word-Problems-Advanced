📖 The Three-Step Arithmancy Method
1

🎨 See It — Visualise

Before writing anything, picture the story in your head. We'll show you the objects from the problem as icons so you can count and compare. This is like drawing a quick sketch before solving.

2

⚗️ Set Up the Spell — Build the Equation

Now translate the story into maths. Choose the operation (+, −, ×, ÷) that matches what's happening in the problem, then fill in the numbers you've identified. This is your equation — the magical formula!

3

🔮 Cast the Spell — Solve & Check

With the equation set up, do the maths and write in your answer. Always ask: "Does my answer make sense?" If you added, the answer should be bigger than both numbers. If you subtracted, it should be smaller.

Why these steps? Most mistakes in word problems happen because students jump straight to arithmetic without understanding the story. By visualising first, you activate your imagination and catch hidden details. By writing the equation second, you separate "what is this asking?" from "how do I calculate it?" — two very different skills!
🔑 Key Words Cheat Sheet
➕ Addition
total, altogether, combined, sum, in all, more, plus, both, added to, and
➖ Subtraction
left, remain, difference, fewer, less than, take away, minus, how many more, lost
✖️ Multiplication
times, each, per, groups of, double, triple, product, every, twice
➗ Division
share, split, each, equal groups, divided by, half, quotient, per
